All National Parks

Jim Corbett National Park – India
India's oldest national park, famous for Bengal tigers, elephants, and diverse wildlife in the Himalayan foothills.

Kaziranga National Park – India
A UNESCO site, home to the world’s largest population of one-horned rhinoceroses, along with elephants and tigers.

Sundarbans National Park – Bangladesh & India
The largest mangrove forest, known for its Royal Bengal tigers and unique tidal ecosystem.

Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park – Indonesia
Features the iconic Mount Bromo volcano, breathtaking sunrise views, and the ancient Tenggerese culture.

Chitwan National Park – Nepal
A diverse jungle habitat with rhinos, tigers, crocodiles, and over 500 bird species, offering jungle safaris and canoe rides.

Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park – Japan
A stunning park encompassing Mount Fuji, hot springs, lakes, and coastal islands with rich biodiversity.

Plitvice Lakes National Park – Croatia
Known for its 16 interconnected lakes with cascading waterfalls, crystal-clear waters, and lush forests.

Saxon Switzerland National Park – Germany
Famous for its dramatic sandstone rock formations, hiking trails, and the Elbe River valley.

Loch Lomond and The Trossachs National Park – Scotland
Features Scotland’s largest freshwater lake, rolling hills, and diverse wildlife like red deer.

Pyrenees National Park – France
A mountainous paradise with scenic valleys, waterfalls, and rare species like the Pyrenean desman.

Jotunheimen National Park – Norway
Home to Norway’s highest peaks, glaciers, and challenging trekking routes like Besseggen Ridge.

Serengeti National Park – Tanzania
Famous for the Great Migration, where millions of wildebeest and zebras travel across the savanna.

Kruger National Park – South Africa
One of Africa’s largest game reserves, home to the Big Five and incredible safari experiences.

Masai Mara National Reserve – Kenya
A vast savanna teeming with lions, elephants, and the Great Migration of wildebeest.

Etosha National Park – Namibia
Features a vast salt pan visible from space and an abundance of wildlife, including rhinos and lions.

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park – Uganda
A dense rainforest home to nearly half of the world’s remaining mountain gorillas.

Yellowstone National Park – USA
The world’s first national park, known for geysers like Old Faithful, hot springs, and abundant wildlife.

Yosemite National Park – USA
Famous for its towering granite cliffs, waterfalls like Yosemite Falls, and ancient giant sequoia trees.

Banff National Park – Canada
A stunning alpine park with turquoise lakes, snow-capped peaks, and a variety of wildlife, including grizzlies.

Grand Canyon National Park – USA
A UNESCO site showcasing a massive canyon carved by the Colorado River over millions of years.

Great Smoky Mountains National Park – USA
A misty mountain range known for diverse plant and animal species, with historical Appalachian culture.

Chichen Itza National Park – Mexico
Home to the famous Mayan pyramid, El Castillo, and other well-preserved ancient structures.

Torres del Paine National Park – Chile
A breathtaking park with granite peaks, glaciers, and vibrant turquoise lakes.

Iguazu National Park – Argentina & Brazil
Home to Iguazu Falls, one of the world’s largest waterfalls, surrounded by lush rainforest.

Los Glaciares National Park – Argentina
Famous for its enormous glaciers, including the spectacular Perito Moreno Glacier.

Manu National Park – Peru
A biodiversity hotspot in the Amazon Rainforest, home to rare species like jaguars and giant otters.

Kakadu National Park – Australia
A diverse landscape with wetlands, Aboriginal rock art, and saltwater crocodiles.

Great Barrier Reef Marine Park – Australia
The world’s largest coral reef system, featuring thousands of marine species and vibrant coral formations.

Fiordland National Park – New Zealand
Known for dramatic fjords, towering waterfalls, and diverse birdlife, including the endangered kiwi.

Tongariro National Park – New Zealand
A UNESCO-listed park with active volcanoes, alpine meadows, and cultural significance to the Maori people.
